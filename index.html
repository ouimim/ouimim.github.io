여기 붙어라
(Yeo-gi But-eo-ra)

X
여 기 붙 어 라 ! (Yeo-Gi But-eo-ra)
Overview

The name of the project, ‘여기 붙어라,’ (Yeo-gi But-eo-ra) comes from a song that Korean children sing when they gather to play in a group.

It is difficult for busy university students or office workers to find mutually available team members or opponents. For this reason, a mobile application was designed in which a chatbot serves as a user’s personal manager and motivator and recommends suitable games for the user’s time and location.

Role

All the members participated in the process of user research, needs-finding and creating solutions. I was in charge of interviews, producing service blueprints and storyboards, and chatbot prototyping using Google Dialogflow.

Time

07/2018 - 08/2018

Project Team

SeongHyun Kim | Hansol Ryu | Joun Baek | MinKyung Choi

Design Process

  • Brainstorming

  • Interview & Survey
    We identified users’ needs by interviewing five university students and surveying 102 people. As a result, we discovered that a more efficient platform is needed for finding sports matches that met users’ criteria.

  • Solutions
    We created potential solutions through the ‘How Might We’ approach. For example, instead of users looking for games themselves, they received personalized recommendations. Moreover, to give users a more personalized and engaging experience, the chatbot interface recommended games in a 1:1 dialog format, gave alerts before games, and accepted post-game feedback.

  • Competing Service Analysis

  • Journey Map + Storyboard

  • Service Blueprint

  • Conversation flow

  • Business Model Canvas + 5 Forces + PEST Analysis

  • Prototyping
    Using Google Dialogflow and Sketch, a prototype of the app’s UI as well as the interaction process with the chatbot interface accessed via a quick menu was created. The GUI and chatbot interface were combined into a list-type interface that enabled users to peruse more information in a glance.

A Dream-Filled Night

A Dream-Filled Night (내가 꿈꾸는 밤)
Project Description

As part of the Art Education Tteam in Art & Sharing, I worked on a project for children in the Children’s Hospital of the National Cancer Center, in which children created their own stories directly on natural-dyed blankets and porcelain dolls. A dye artist Jeong-Sun Lee, parents of beneficiary children, and employees of medical social workers participated throughout the project.

Children hospitalized with long-term illness have fewer opportunities to experience various cultural activities. Also, it is difficult for them to hang out with other friends, as most of the time, they only focus on treatments. Therefore, this project provided children with opportunities to feel a sense of accomplishment through creating their own artwork. Moreover, children said that they were highly satisfied with this activity because they could spend time with other children.

Arts Council Korea

One Fine Day - Stealing Your Day Away
(어느 멋진 날 - 너의 하루를 훔치다)
Project Description

As part of the Art Education Team in Art & Sharing, I worked on a project to bring artistic enjoyment to the daily lives of high school students who were busy with college entrance exams. High school students spend most of their time in school with classes and self-study even until late at night. To make matters worse, art-based classes are replaced by self-study time for major subjects, so students hardly have time to freely engage in cultural and artistic activities.

With the aid of letters written by students living in dorms and filling their daily lives with nothing but study, we transformed their feelings and words into stories. Then, we illustrated those stories and made them into lamp shades. Also, for students to release their troubles, we engaged them with action-painting and displayed the results via banners.

Students from Goyang Foreign Language High School radio broadcasting station contributed to the project preparation process with us. Also, students, being inspired by this project, voluntarily expanded it to hold a small music concert on weekends. Students said that they were highly satisfied because they were able to escape the monotonous life in school and have a new and freeing experience.

Where Should I Open A Cafe?

Where Should I Open A Cafe?
(까페,어디에 차리면 좋을까요?)
Project Description

In ‘Workshop D’ of the Seoul Information Design Lab, I learned the concepts and processes of information design and practiced communication methods and visualization technologies, and used this knowledge and experience to conduct a study on the feasibility of opening a coffee shop in Seoul.

Coffee is very popular in Korea, with as many as 500 cups of coffee drank per person per year. However, cafes have higher start-up rates but significantly lower survival rates compared to other businesses. In order to provide useful information to those who want to start coffee shops, we conducted analysis by ‘dong’ based on three indicators: the number of cafes, the floating population, and public land prices. Then, we examined the pattern of those data.
(*Sales indicators are excluded because they are not listed in the public data portal, and data from credit card companies cannot be analyzed by ‘dong’.)

We found that areas with the highest concentration of cafes in Seoul are quite consistent with areas classified as gentrification areas. Therefore, we analyzed the patterns of the three indicators from 2008 to 2016 using R, by selecting 12 dongs classified as gentrification areas. As a result, we took a pattern-based trend of six neighborhoods from areas where the floating population is rising, the public land prices are low, and the number of cafes is low.

Institution

Seoul Information Design Lab

Time
